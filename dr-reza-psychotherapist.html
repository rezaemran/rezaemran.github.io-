About Me

I am Dr. Reza, a clinical psychologist with a passion for helping individuals struggling with addiction and mental health challenges. With a background in clinical psychology and an M.Sc. in the field, I bring a blend of professional experience, academic expertise, and empathy to my practice. Currently, I work as a psychotherapist and a school counselor, where I provide guidance and support to individuals across various age groups, with a particular focus on addiction, cognitive training, and behavioral therapy.

I have developed a specialized application called "Neuro Rehab," featuring cognitive training games that help individuals with addiction improve their working memory and attention control. Additionally, I have translated significant psychological texts, such as "Psychoanalysis and Faith," deepening my understanding of psychoanalysis and therapeutic faith.

Services

  • Individual Psychotherapy: Personalized one-on-one therapy sessions aimed at addressing mental health challenges, anxiety, depression, and addiction recovery.
  • Addiction Counseling: Comprehensive support and therapy for individuals facing addiction issues, including the use of cognitive-behavioral techniques and approach-avoidance training.
  • School Counseling: Counseling services for high school students, helping them navigate personal, academic, and mental health challenges.
  • Neuro Rehab App: A cognitive rehabilitation program that aids in enhancing working memory and attention control in individuals overcoming addiction.
  • Approach-Avoidance Training: A unique therapy service designed to assist individuals in overcoming cravings and negative patterns related to addiction.

Publications

  • Neuro Rehab: A Cognitive Approach to Addiction Recovery - A paper outlining the development and clinical impact of my Neuro Rehab application.
  • Psychoanalysis and Faith: Freud-Fister Correspondence - Translated the critical correspondence between Sigmund Freud and Oscar Fister, offering insights into the relationship between psychoanalysis and faith.
  • The Role of Cognitive Training in Addiction Therapy - A publication discussing the efficacy of cognitive training in enhancing recovery outcomes in individuals with addiction.
